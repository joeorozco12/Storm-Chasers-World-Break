# Storm Chasers: World Break - Complete Foundation

## SECTION 1: GAME VISION
- **Polished one-paragraph game pitch:**
  *Storm Chasers: World Break is a cinematic social survival-adventure on Roblox where living weather transforms the map in real time, turning every session into a hunt for danger, discovery, and spectacle. Players explore a vast storm-scarred world, forecast and chase rare weather fronts, encounter mythical storm creatures, build shelters that must withstand the elements, and trigger shared sky events that make the whole server stop and stare. The game is easy to understand in minutes—watch the sky, follow the storm, survive the impact, discover what it revealed—but deep enough to support creature mastery, base engineering, exploration prestige, and memorable multiplayer stories.*
- **Genre blend:** Survival adventure + creature discovery/capture + co-op world event game + light base building + atmospheric exploration.
- **Target audience:** Ages 9-18 core Roblox audience, plus spectacle-driven social players, collectors, exploration fans, and creators looking for clip-worthy moments.
- **Emotional pillars:** Wonder, tension, awe, curiosity, mastery, shared survival, and prestige through discovery.
- **What makes it different from common Roblox games:**
  - The weather is the main gameplay system, not a backdrop.
  - Server-wide storms physically and visually reshape routes, resources, and creature availability.
  - Progress is driven by discovery, preparation, and coordination rather than repetitive clicking.
  - Monetization emphasizes shared spectacle and expression instead of stat inflation.
- **Why it could become highly shareable:**
  - Storms create naturally cinematic moments for thumbnails and short-form clips.
  - Rare weather + creature spawns drive “get in my server now” urgency.
  - Social purchases create giant visible moments everyone can enjoy.
  - Players can show off bases, discoveries, and emergent storm stories.

## SECTION 2: CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
- **First-session loop:** Spawn into a calm valley hub, get a simple forecast tool, see a distant storm wall forming, run toward an event marker with other players, gather storm-touched materials, witness a rare creature silhouette in the clouds, scramble back to shelter, and return with a new journal entry and a stronger desire to chase the next storm.
- **Mid-game loop:** Predict useful storms, travel to target biomes, deploy tracking tools, study/tag/calm/capture creatures, gather weather-specific resources, reinforce a base for upcoming hazards, and unlock improved forecasting and mobility tech.
- **Long-term retention loop:** Complete biome journals, optimize shelters for specialized weather, master hybrid storm hunts, collect prestige cosmetics, host spectacle events, discover secret world-break phenomena, and participate in limited community weather seasons.
- **Co-op/social loop:** Players split roles between forecasting, scouting, luring, photographing, shelter support, and creature response. Group success increases survival, creature encounter quality, and event completion rewards.
- **Risk/reward structure:** The rarer and more unstable the storm, the more valuable the materials, discoveries, and creature encounters—but storms can destroy weak structures, knock players off routes, reduce visibility, and force hard decisions around extraction versus greed.

## SECTION 3: WORLD DESIGN
- **Map concept:** A large ring-shaped continent surrounding a shattered atmospheric scar called the Skybreak Expanse. Safe travel routes connect outer biomes, while inner regions become increasingly volatile and reward-rich as weather intensity escalates.
- **Biome list:**
  1. **Starter Basin** - calm meadows, tutorial shelters, rolling hills.
  2. **Thunderstep Plains** - open grasslands, lightning rods, roaming storm herds.
  3. **Glassreef Coast** - reflective beaches, tide caves, storm-polished crystal shelves.
  4. **Mistwood Canopy** - dense luminous forest, heavy fog, vertical traversal.
  5. **Redstone Badlands** - cracked mesas, dust funnels, exposed ruins.
  6. **Frostveil Peaks** - icy cliffs, whiteout storms, aurora nights.
  7. **Sunken Bloom Marsh** - flooded wetlands, spores, glowing reeds.
  8. **Skybreak Expanse** - the central rupture zone, floating debris, legendary storms.
- **Weather by biome:**
  - Starter Basin: light rain, soft fog, breezes.
  - Thunderstep Plains: electrical storms, wind squalls, hail.
  - Glassreef Coast: cyclones, glowing rain, storm surge.
  - Mistwood Canopy: dense fog, pollen storms, luminous drizzle.
  - Redstone Badlands: dust storms, heatbursts, dry lightning.
  - Frostveil Peaks: blizzards, auroras, ice thunder.
  - Sunken Bloom Marsh: acid mist, swamp thunder, bioluminescent rain.
  - Skybreak Expanse: eclipses, meteor squalls, rift storms, legendary hybrid events.
- **Notable landmarks:** Skyglass Lighthouse, the Coil Spire, Hollow Titan ribcage, Aurora Observatory, Flooded Bell Tower, the Split Mesa, the Mirror Flats, and the Worldbreak crater rim.
- **Visual distinction:**
  - Plains use broad silhouettes and visible storm movement.
  - Coast emphasizes reflections, horizon drama, and lightning over water.
  - Mistwood uses volumetric layers and glowing flora.
  - Badlands use hard shadows, warm/cold contrast, and dust veils.
  - Peaks prioritize high-contrast snow, aurora color, and storm glow.
  - Marsh features luminous waterlines and eerie fog.
  - Expanse uses impossible sky geometry and floating shards.
- **Wow moment locations:**
  - The first view of the Skybreak Expanse from an elevated ridge.
  - A hidden cave whose crystals flash with each lightning strike.
  - An aurora arch over Frostveil’s highest point.
  - A moonlit marsh clearing where glowing rain reveals invisible footprints.
  - The Coil Spire during a Lightning Serpent encounter.

## SECTION 4: WEATHER SYSTEM DESIGN
- **Weather event types (12+):**
  1. **Passing Rain** - normal; wets surfaces, boosts crops, reveals tracks.
  2. **Heavy Thunderstorm** - normal; lightning hazards, creature agitation.
  3. **Wind Squall** - normal; glider boost, loose-object hazards.
  4. **Dense Fog Bank** - normal; stealth/discovery opportunities, low visibility.
  5. **Dust Funnel** - rare; moves debris, uncovers ruins.
  6. **Hail Burst** - rare; structure chip damage, rare ice shards.
  7. **Storm Surge** - rare; floods lowlands, opens coastal paths.
  8. **Blizzard** - rare; movement drain, hidden frost creatures.
  9. **Glowing Rain** - rare; bioluminescent surfaces, rare nocturnal spawns.
  10. **Aurora Night** - rare; boosts tracking tools, serene social gathering.
  11. **Meteor Shower** - legendary; impact resources, crater events.
  12. **Solar Eclipse** - legendary; shadows awaken creatures, server-wide spectacle.
  13. **Rift Tempest** - legendary; floating debris, unstable gravity pockets.
  14. **Cataclysmic Lightning Front** - legendary; Lightning Serpent primary spawn event.
- **Rarity tiers:** Normal, Rare, Legendary.
- **Gameplay impact:** Weather changes traversal, visibility, resource nodes, creature behavior, building stress, and event activation windows.
- **Environmental changes:** Wet materials become reflective, trees sway, debris moves, fog layers thicken, rivers flood, snow accumulates, and lighting/post-processing shifts dramatically.
- **Normal vs rare vs legendary:**
  - Normal: Passing Rain, Heavy Thunderstorm, Wind Squall, Dense Fog Bank.
  - Rare: Dust Funnel, Hail Burst, Storm Surge, Blizzard, Glowing Rain, Aurora Night.
  - Legendary: Meteor Shower, Solar Eclipse, Rift Tempest, Cataclysmic Lightning Front.
- **Hybrid events:**
  - Thunderstorm + Wind Squall = Supercell.
  - Storm Surge + Glowing Rain = Luminous Tide.
  - Blizzard + Aurora Night = Prism Whiteout.
  - Dust Funnel + Dry Lightning = Ember Cyclone.
  - Solar Eclipse + Rift Tempest = Voidbreak Event.

## SECTION 5: CREATURE SYSTEM
- **Player verbs:** Track, study, photograph, tag, calm, rescue, capture, and bond.
- **Creature progression:** First discovery adds a journal entry, repeated interaction unlocks behavior data, successful calm/capture unlocks utility traits or cosmetic companion forms, and high-end mastery unlocks storm forecasting bonuses and prestige titles.
- **Creatures (10+):**
  1. **Lightning Serpent** - flagship legendary; appears during Cataclysmic Lightning Fronts around spires and open plains. It coils through clouds, dives like a living bolt, briefly silhouettes the sky, and forces players to ground conductive objects, use surge anchors, and coordinate tagging or calming windows. Encounter goal: survive, document, then attempt a synchronized bind/calm sequence.
  2. **Rainback Tortoise** - common; spawns after rain near wetlands; passive, leaves collectible moss trails.
  3. **Static Hare** - uncommon; heavy thunderstorm spawn in grasslands; skittish and teleports short distances with sparks.
  4. **Fog Manta** - uncommon; dense fog over cliffs; glides silently and can be photographed for mapping upgrades.
  5. **Cinder Vulture** - rare; dry lightning in badlands; circles exposed ruins and guards ember shards.
  6. **Glint Crab** - common/uncommon; storm surge coastlines; burrows in reflective sand and reveals hidden caches.
  7. **Aurora Stag** - rare; aurora nights in Frostveil; elegant herd creature tied to prestige cosmetics.
  8. **Bloom Leech** - uncommon; glowing rain in marshes; follows light sources and can be repelled or studied.
  9. **Hailjaw Ray** - rare; hail storms over coasts or peaks; aggressive aerial swoops.
  10. **Rift Owl** - rare; eclipse or rift tempests; warns of unstable anomalies.
  11. **Meteor Mole** - rare; meteor showers; digs up impact cores.
  12. **Thunderhoof** - rare herd beast; supercells in plains; can power storm batteries if calmed.
- **Lightning Serpent detail:**
  - **Visual identity:** A long translucent body with a bright core, forked antler fins, and delayed afterimage trails.
  - **Behavior:** Orbits charged landmarks, strikes at tall metal objects, vanishes into clouds, then dives unpredictably.
  - **Multiplayer moment:** One player spots it first, sirens trigger, the sky flashes, and everyone nearby can see the encounter from huge distance.
  - **Reward design:** Journal prestige, rare materials, cosmetic serpent-themed base accents, and one of the most iconic clips in the game.

## SECTION 6: BUILDING / BASE SYSTEM
- **Philosophy:** Bases are expressive storm shelters and social showcases, not grind-heavy fortress chores.
- **How weather threatens weak builds:** Wind tears off unsecured modules, lightning overloads exposed systems, floodwater damages floor-level utilities, hail chips weak roofing, and legendary events temporarily disable poorly grounded devices.
- **How players defend:** Use anchors, storm shutters, grounding rods, insulated walls, flood stilts, drainage modules, and weather-specific reinforcement plates.
- **Storm-resistant materials/modules:** Treated Pine, Sealstone Panels, Copper Ground Rods, Glassreef Composite, Aurora Weave Insulation, Marsh Resin Sealant, Storm Battery, Pressure Vent, Elevated Foundation, and Lightning Lure Decoy.
- **Why bases stay fun:** Repairs are low-friction, most damage is partial rather than full loss, blueprint snapping is simple, and weather-resistant upgrades feel like meaningful expression and mastery rather than punishment.

## SECTION 10: ROBLOX TECH ARCHITECTURE
- **Workspace organization:** Streamed terrain, biome folders, landmark models, active weather zones, encounter spawn points, interactables, and temporary event models.
- **ReplicatedStorage organization:** Shared config, enums, utility modules, remotes, client-safe weather state snapshots, and creature definitions.
- **ServerScriptService organization:** Authoritative systems for weather, scheduler, creature spawning, AI orchestration, monetization products, and data services.
- **StarterPlayer organization:** Player controllers for HUD, storm tracker, camera FX, interaction prompts, and local audio/VFX.
- **StarterGui organization:** Minimalist HUD, event banner, journal, build mode overlay, store/spectacle panel, and alerts.
- **Modules vs scripts vs local scripts:**
  - ModuleScripts for reusable logic/config.
  - Scripts for server bootstraps and listeners.
  - LocalScripts for presentation, client prediction of UI only, and camera/audio polish.
- **Remote events / remote functions needed:** WeatherStateUpdated, StormEventAnnounce, CreatureEncounterStarted, TriggerStormSeed, RequestForecast, BuildActionRequest, JournalUpdated, PurchaseFeedback.
- **Naming conventions:** PascalCase for modules/classes, camelCase for local variables/functions, SCREAMING_SNAKE_CASE for constants, descriptive folders grouped by domain.
- **Data persistence:** Profile-style keyed data with versioned schema, autosave, transactional unlock writes, and event journals stored as compact dictionaries.
- **Performance/replication:** Server owns event selection and spawn states; clients receive compact state packets and create local-only VFX layers. Use streaming-enabled world partitioning and pooled effects.
- **Anti-exploit:** Validate all purchase triggers, capture requests, build placement, and journal unlocks on the server; never trust client storm state or creature outcomes.
- **Server-authoritative weather:** Scheduler chooses events, WeatherManager owns active states, clients only render replicated weather descriptors and request interactions through remotes.

## SECTION 12: MVP PLAN
- **Smallest fun playable version:** One highly polished valley + plains region, 4 weather types, one shelter plot, 3 creatures, one server-wide storm spectacle, and a simplified Lightning Serpent encounter.
- **Exact features to build first:**
  - Weather scheduler with clear visual state changes.
  - Traversable map slice with strong landmarks.
  - Basic shelter building snap system.
  - Journal + discovery rewards.
  - Lightning storm + Lightning Serpent encounter.
  - One social monetization trigger: Storm Seed.
- **Postpone:** Full base complexity, hybrid weather combinations, advanced AI variations, full biome roster, long tech tree, and broad live-ops.
- **Essential for first public prototype:** Immediate visual wow, readable storm objective, one group event worth talking about, and one clear progression hook after the first encounter.
- **Milestones:**
  - **Phase 1: first playable** - one zone, rain/thunder/fog, journal, shelter, basic resources.
  - **Phase 2: fun loop** - rare creatures, stronger storm response, co-op rewards, first hybrid event.
  - **Phase 3: monetization and polish** - spectacle purchases, photo mode, base cosmetics, stronger VFX/audio.
  - **Phase 4: content expansion** - more biomes, legendary events, seasonal progression, prestige housing.

## SECTION 14: UI / UX PLAN
- **HUD:** Minimal compass, weather icon/intensity, shelter integrity, and quick action slots.
- **Storm tracker:** An expandable radar ring showing incoming fronts, rarity color, and target biome.
- **Creature alert system:** High-priority cinematic banner with creature silhouette, location hint, and timer.
- **Inventory/journal:** Card-based illustrated journal with tabs for creatures, weather, landmarks, and discoveries.
- **Monetization surfaces:** Event Observatory menu for spectacle purchases framed as celebratory server actions, not power boosts.
- **Event notifications:** Short, cinematic, full-width alerts with large iconography and ambient audio stings.
- **Beauty/uncluttered approach:** Keep default HUD small and translucent, reveal detailed panels on demand, and let the world remain the star.

## SECTION 15: ART DIRECTION GUIDE
- **Color palette:** Calm scenes use cool blue-greens and warm golds; storm states push into deep indigo, electric cyan, saturated violet, sulfuric green, and occasional ember orange.
- **Lighting mood:** Dynamic day-night transitions, strong god rays, heavy storm contrast, and dramatic lightning flashes that briefly reshape scene readability.
- **Materials:** Wet rock, glossy mud, reflective puddles, matte grasses, translucent crystals, and weathered metal.
- **Particles:** Layered, directional, high-readability particles with occasional oversized hero effects for legendary events.
- **Creature silhouettes:** Readable from far distance, asymmetrical accents, glowing internal lines, and unique movement outlines.
- **Environment storytelling:** Show past storms through broken towers, grounded cables, stranded ships, fossilized roots, and abandoned observatories.
- **Thumbnail/key art ideas:** Lightning Serpent over the Coil Spire, squad running through reflective flooded plains, aurora-lit base on a cliff, and meteor impacts near the Skybreak crater.

## SECTION 16: RISKS AND SOLUTIONS
- **Biggest design risks:**
  - Too many systems at launch -> focus on one excellent storm loop.
  - Base building becoming chores -> make shelter upgrades impactful but low-friction.
  - Creature system lacking clarity -> use clear spawn telegraphing and journal guidance.
- **Biggest technical risks:**
  - Weather VFX overwhelming clients -> keep heavy particles local and LOD-driven.
  - AI desync in storms -> server owns core state, clients interpolate presentation only.
  - Open world performance -> stream terrain and spawn only nearby active actors.
- **Biggest monetization risks:**
  - Players perceiving event triggers as paid access to fun -> guarantee strong natural event cadence.
  - Convenience drifting into advantage -> ensure all functional items have earnable equivalents.
- **Reduce scope while keeping vision strong:** Launch with one unforgettable biome pair, one shelter loop, one legendary creature, and one premium spectacle system.
